The six that matter

Technique before strength

Most plateaus aren't a lack of power — they're wasted energy. These are the habits that separate flailing from flowing. Read them, then feel them in the demo below.

01

Trust your feet

Place the inside edge or big-toe point on the hold and commit your weight to it. Legs are your strongest muscles — let them carry you while your hands mostly steer.

Look at the foothold until your toe lands on it.
02

Center of mass over feet

Balance holds when your hips stack above your base of support — the space between your feet. Drift outside it and you swing, grip, and burn out.

Feel weight pressing down through your toes, not out on your hands.
03

Straight-arm resting

Hang from your skeleton, not your biceps. Locked-off arms pump you out; straight arms let bone and tendon take the load so muscle recovers.

On any decent hold, drop your shoulders and hang.
04

Hips to the wall

Turning a hip in ("backstepping") brings your center of mass close to the rock and extends your reach. The further your hips sit out, the more your arms fight gravity.

Twist so one shoulder points at the next hold.
05

Flag to stay square

When you only have one good foothold, extend the free leg out as a counterweight. Flagging shifts your balance point so you don't barn-door off the wall.

Press the flagging foot lightly against the wall for tension.
06

Quiet feet

Precise, silent placements mean you're looking and committing. Scrabbling feet mean you're grabbing with your hands and hoping. Move deliberately.

Aim for no sound when your shoe touches the hold.

Interactive · Physics sandbox

Set a stance, watch the strain

Grip holds with his hands, stand his feet on holds, and drag his hips to set a stance — the hips hold that position, so standing taller lets his arms reach farther. The panel shows the strain in each arm. Lean off-balance or hang from one arm and that arm climbs over its strength: hold it there for five seconds and it gives out — and he falls. Limbs are a fixed length (a hold out of reach stays out of reach), and reach too far off a foot and it will slip.

Drag limbs onto holds · release off a hold to let it dangle
